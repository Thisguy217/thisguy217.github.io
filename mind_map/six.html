When the reached the region of the Black Sea (Ukraine), the tribes already in the region (Ostrogoths, Visigoths, and Vandals) fled west. The Huns became invaders, clashing with the roman empire and with tribes in the western Europe. The Huns, led by the great warrior Attila, crossed the Rhine in 452. They made it all the way into modern France, where the Franks finally defeated them at Chalons, less than 200 miles was of Paris.
